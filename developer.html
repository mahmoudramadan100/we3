WHAT TO EXPECT

THE ROLE

At Jam3, you'll be exposed to every discipline in our creative process – UX, UI, interactive design, motion design, and copywriting. You’ll be part of a dynamic team that's made up of likeminded, talented people. With them, you'll work hard to create something that will - without a doubt - stand out in your portfolio.

addition to your team project, you’ll be lending a hand to the creative team. Participating in brainstorm sessions, building winning pitches, preparing assets and design bibles for productions, you'll be in the thick of it all.

Oh, and you’ll probably make a friend or ten. NBD role image for the designer

REQUIREMENTS

You own your shit. You’re a self-starter who shows passion and dedication to every task given. "Make it happen and give all the fucks", is what we like to say–and the attitude we need to see from you. Here is a straightforward breakdown:

  • You play nice with other people.
  • You’re an actively engaged person. That means you are present, motivated, and social.
  • You excel at Photoshop (Bonus points for Illustrator, InDesign, Cinema4D, After Effects, etc). You can create initial design layouts with confidence and know how to effectively communicate concepts to everyone on your team.
  • You have a solid understanding of visual hierarchy and colour theory.
  • You will assist with miscellaneous front-end build production if needed (slicing, layout, etc.).
  • You have creative courage. This is a tough industry, only the brave and sturdy remain. Be prepared to defend your decisions and take constructive criticism.

To us, beauty without substance has no value. At Jam3, design thinking and execution are equally important, so when we’re examining your portfolio we’ll be on the lookout for not only the how but the why.

LIFE AT JAM3

Here's a little peek of how your week could play out:

  • Scrum with your teammates – folks like executive producers, coordinators, senior designers, and devs
  • A caffeine-fuelled brainstorm session.
  • Building a pitch deck for a new project.
  • Rapid prototyping a website concept YOU helped to create.
  • Designer's Weekly Critique – a meeting with the entire creative team, where everyone can share what they've been working on, and get feedback from the team
  • Grabbing lunch with your colleagues. Laughter and debates ensue
  • On Fridays, we host Beer O’Clock. It’s an opportunity to grab a drink with your fellow Jamsters, revel in the week’s victories, and swap lessons and resources.

FREQUENTLY
ASKED QUESTIONS

WHAT IS THE WE3 PROGRAM ALL ABOUT?

School is great, but school (like a stolen car) can only take you so far. The WE3 program lets you take the skills you’ve learned in school and put them to work in a real-world environment – with a real team, real clients, and real stakes.

HOW MANY INTERNSHIP POSITIONS ARE AVAILABLE?

We hire 2 designer interns and 2 developer interns.

WHAT WILL I BE WORKING ON?

In short, everything. We’ll start you out on deep concepting, brainstorming, rapid prototyping, and internal projects, before throwing you at some real projects. Like, seriously awesome internet-exploding projects.

IS THE INTERNSHIP PAID OR UNPAID?

Are unpaid internships still a thing? At Jam3, we value your time and creativity, and we believe that all internships should be paid at a fair wage.

IS THE INTERNSHIP PAID OR UNPAID?

Ideally, our internship cycle is 16 weeks long. This gives our interns a chance to experience the many stages of a project while in production. We do however, recognize that some people have specific program requirements, visa requirements, etc. please indicate this in your application. If you’re selected we’ll work on a plan that’s appropriate for your situation.

ARE THE DATES FLEXIBLE?

Yes, we understand that program start dates vary, we can accommodate on a case-by-case basis, but please mention this in your application.

DO I HAVE TO BE A CURRENT STUDENT?

Applicants should be recent graduates (up to 2 years post-graduation) or currently enrolled in a program.

CAN I APPLY IF I AM AN INTERNATIONAL STUDENT?

Absolutely! International Experience Canada (IEC) is a program designed to allow youth the opportunity to work in Canada for temporary periods. Citizens of countries with a bilateral youth mobility arrangement with Canada who are between 18 and 35 years old may be eligible for IEC work permits.

The International Co-op Internship category is for you if:

  • You’re a student registered at a post-secondary institution
  • You have a job offer for a work placement or internship in Canada
  • You need to do this work placement or internship to complete your studies
  • You’ll work for the same employer in the same location during your stay in Canada

WHAT COUNTRIES HAVE A BILATERAL YOUTH MOBILITY ARRANGEMENT WITH CANADA FOR INTERNATIONAL CO-OP INTERNSHIPS?

Australia, Austria, Chile, Costa Rica, Croatia, Czech Republic, Estonia, France, Germany, Greece, Ireland, Latvia Republic, Lithuania, Mexico, Norway, Poland, Slovakia, Slovenia, Span, Sweden, Switzerland, Taiwan, Ukraine

WHAT IF I DON'T QUALIFY FOR INTERNATIONAL EXPERIENCE CANADA, SHOULD I STILL APPLY?

Absolutely! We will do our best to review each international application to see what we can do
