WHAT TO EXPECT

THE ROLE

At Jam3, you'll be exposed to every discipline in our creative process – UX, UI, interactive design, motion design, and copywriting. You’ll be part of a dynamic team that's made up of likeminded, talented people. With them, you'll work hard to create something that will - without a doubt - stand out in your portfolio.

addition to your team project, you’ll be lending a hand to the creative team. Participating in brainstorm sessions, building winning pitches, preparing assets and design bibles for productions, you'll be in the thick of it all.

Oh, and you’ll probably make a friend or ten. NBD role image for the designer

REQUIREMENTS

You own your shit. You’re a self-starter who shows passion and dedication to every task given. "Make it happen and give all the fucks", is what we like to say–and the attitude we need to see from you. Here is a straightforward breakdown:

  • You play nice with other people.
  • You’re an actively engaged person. That means you are present, motivated, and social.
  • You excel at Photoshop (Bonus points for Illustrator, InDesign, Cinema4D, After Effects, etc). You can create initial design layouts with confidence and know how to effectively communicate concepts to everyone on your team.
  • You have a solid understanding of visual hierarchy and colour theory.
  • You will assist with miscellaneous front-end build production if needed (slicing, layout, etc.).
  • You have creative courage. This is a tough industry, only the brave and sturdy remain. Be prepared to defend your decisions and take constructive criticism.

To us, beauty without substance has no value. At Jam3, design thinking and execution are equally important, so when we’re examining your portfolio we’ll be on the lookout for not only the how but the why.

LIFE AT JAM3

Here's a little peek of how your week could play out:

  • Scrum with your teammates – folks like executive producers, coordinators, senior designers, and devs
  • A caffeine-fuelled brainstorm session.
  • Building a pitch deck for a new project.
  • Rapid prototyping a website concept YOU helped to create.
  • Designer's Weekly Critique – a meeting with the entire creative team, where everyone can share what they've been working on, and get feedback from the team
  • Grabbing lunch with your colleagues. Laughter and debates ensue
  • On Fridays, we host Beer O’Clock. It’s an opportunity to grab a drink with your fellow Jamsters, revel in the week’s victories, and swap lessons and resources.
